Artificial Intelligence represents a transformative field of computer science focused on creating systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. These tasks include learning from experience, recognizing patterns, understanding natural language, making decisions, and solving complex problems. Modern AI systems leverage machine learning algorithms, particularly deep learning neural networks, to process vast amounts of data and identify intricate relationships that would be impossible for humans to detect manually. From virtual assistants like Siri and Alexa to autonomous vehicles and medical diagnosis systems, AI applications are rapidly reshaping industries and daily life.

The evolution of AI has progressed through several distinct phases, from early rule-based expert systems to today's sophisticated neural networks and large language models. Machine learning, a subset of AI, enables computers to improve their performance on tasks through experience without being explicitly programmed for every scenario. Deep learning, which uses multi-layered neural networks inspired by the human brain, has achieved remarkable breakthroughs in image recognition, natural language processing, and game playing. As AI continues to advance, researchers are exploring artificial general intelligence (AGI) - systems that could match or exceed human cognitive abilities across a wide range of tasks - while also addressing critical concerns about ethics, bias, transparency, and the societal impact of increasingly powerful AI systems.
