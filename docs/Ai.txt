Deep learning is a subset of machine learning that uses artificial neural networks with multiple layers to learn hierarchical representations of data. The depth of these networks allows them to automatically discover intricate patterns and features from raw data without manual feature engineering. Deep learning models excel at tasks involving unstructured data like images, audio, and text. The breakthrough came with improved training techniques, larger datasets, and powerful GPUs that made training deep networks feasible.

Convolutional neural networks revolutionized computer vision by using convolutional layers that preserve spatial relationships in images. Recurrent neural networks and their variants like LSTMs handle sequential data with temporal dependencies. Transformer architectures with self-attention mechanisms have become the foundation for modern natural language processing. Training deep networks requires careful initialization, normalization techniques, and regularization to prevent overfitting. Transfer learning allows pre-trained models to be fine-tuned for specific tasks with limited data, democratizing access to powerful AI capabilities across various domains.
