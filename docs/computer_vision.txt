Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence that trains computers to interpret and understand visual information from the world. It involves acquiring, processing, analyzing, and understanding digital images and videos to extract meaningful information. Computer vision systems use convolutional neural networks that apply filters to detect edges, textures, and patterns at different scales. These networks learn hierarchical features automatically, from simple edges in early layers to complex objects in deeper layers.

Applications of computer vision span numerous industries including autonomous vehicles that detect pedestrians and traffic signs, medical imaging systems that identify tumors and diseases, facial recognition for security and authentication, and augmented reality that overlays digital content on the real world. Key tasks include image classification, object detection, semantic segmentation, instance segmentation, and pose estimation. Modern architectures like ResNet, YOLO, and Vision Transformers have pushed the boundaries of accuracy and speed. Challenges include handling occlusion, varying lighting conditions, different viewpoints, and ensuring robustness in real-world scenarios.
